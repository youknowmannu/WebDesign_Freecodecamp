Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist and humanitarian who led the Green Revolution, a remarkable initiative that increased global food production and saved millions from famine.

Born on March 25, 1914, in Cresco, Iowa, Dr. Borlaug's work in developing high-yielding and disease-resistant wheat varieties significantly improved agricultural productivity in developing countries, particularly in India, Pakistan, and Mexico.

For his extraordinary contributions to humanity, he was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970.
